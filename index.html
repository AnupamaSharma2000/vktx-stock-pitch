01 — INVESTMENT THESIS

Why the Market Is Wrong

VKTX has fallen ~67% from its 2024 peak of $99, not because the science failed — but because macro fears and competitive noise crowded out rational analysis. The Phase 3 VANQUISH trials just completed enrollment, and oral VK2735 Phase 3 is underway. This is maximum fear, minimum information — the ideal entry point.

Non-Consensus Insight
The market is pricing VKTX as a single-drug injectable story. It is not.
Sell-side consensus focuses on the injectable VK2735 Phase 3 readout as the binary event. But this framing misses the oral VK2735 TAM — a structurally distinct and largely unpriced market segment. Approximately 60% of obesity-eligible patients are needle-averse or actively refuse injectable therapy. No approved oral GLP-1 exists today at the efficacy level VK2735 Oral has demonstrated (12.2% at 13 weeks). Lilly’s orforglipron has shown comparable efficacy but significant liver enzyme concerns that may limit its label. Novo’s oral semaglutide (Rybelsus) requires highly specific administration conditions, limiting real-world uptake. VK2735 Oral is not a backup to the injectable — it is access to a 60M+ patient segment that the injectable cannot reach. This program is currently embedded in VKTX at near-zero value by the market. The optionality alone justifies a $5–10/share premium above our base case. Falsifiability: This thesis is wrong if VANQUISH-1 interim data (Q2 2026) shows <10% weight loss at 13 weeks OR if the FDA raises a clinical hold on VK2735 ahead of the NDA. Either event would trigger immediate position re-evaluation.

Follow-up query
“VK2735 oral Phase 2 results weight loss percentage — what did VENTURE-Oral show”

Data corrected Initial data point in our model was 8.2% — sourced from an early Phase 1 28-day study. The definitive Phase 2 VENTURE-Oral result (Aug 2025, PR Newswire) showed up to 12.2% body weight reduction at 13 weeks, all doses statistically significant vs. placebo. The Phase 1 figure was outdated. Model updated.
Follow-up query
“Lilly orforglipron liver enzyme safety concerns FDA — what is the current status”
Competitive insight
Lilly's orforglipron Phase 3 has shown dose-dependent liver enzyme elevations (ALT/AST increases) in a subset of participants. While not a clinical hold, this is a known label risk that Lilly is managing with monitoring protocols. Rybelsus (oral semaglutide) requires fasting 30 minutes before dosing with ≤120mL water — limiting real-world adherence. Neither competitor offers the convenience profile of VK2735 Oral.
Non-consensus insight derived
The oral GLP-1 market is NOT a backup story for VKTX — it is access to a structurally distinct patient segment (60M+ needle-averse) that is currently UNADDRESSED by any approved therapy. The sell-side models this as secondary upside. We model it as a separate program with independent rNPV ($2.1B), because it serves a different patient. This reframing is the differentiated thesis element.
